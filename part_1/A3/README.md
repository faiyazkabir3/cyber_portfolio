## Activity Description
This activity investigates how cybersecurity and information security principles are applied in a home environment. The focus is on identifying examples of the **CIA triad (Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability)** within everyday household technologies and systems.

## Security Concept: CIA Triad
The CIA triad is a fundamental model in cybersecurity used to guide the design and protection of information systems. It includes three key principles:

- **Confidentiality** – ensuring sensitive information is only accessible to authorized users.
- **Integrity** – ensuring data and systems cannot be modified improperly.
- **Availability** – ensuring systems and services remain accessible when needed.

These principles are widely used when designing secure digital environments (Stallings & Brown, 2018).

---

## Discoveries in the Home Environment

### Confidentiality
Confidentiality ensures that personal information and digital systems are protected from unauthorized access.

Examples observed in a home environment include:

- Home Wi-Fi networks are protected with passwords and encryption protocols such as **WPA2 or WPA3** to prevent unauthorized access (Australian Cyber Security Centre, 2023).
- Personal devices such as laptops and smartphones are secured using **passwords, PIN codes, or biometric authentication** such as fingerprint or facial recognition.
- Financial records and personal documents are stored securely either through **physical storage (locked cabinets)** or **encrypted digital storage**.

### Integrity
Integrity ensures that home devices and data cannot be modified without authorization.

Examples include:

- Antivirus and anti-malware software protect computers from malicious software that may alter files or system configurations.
- Smart home devices such as security cameras or routers use authentication and firmware updates to prevent unauthorized access or tampering.

### Availability
Availability ensures that household technology and services remain accessible when needed.

Examples include:

- Home internet connections support daily activities such as work, study, and communication.
- Home security systems remain operational to provide continuous monitoring and protection.
- Backup power solutions such as **Uninterruptible Power Supplies (UPS)** help maintain availability of important devices during power outages.
