# A1 – Security Concepts Used on Campus

## Activity Description
This activity investigates how fundamental cybersecurity principles are applied within a university campus environment. The focus is on identifying examples of the **CIA triad**, which consists of confidentiality, integrity, and availability.

## Security Concept: CIA Triad
The CIA triad is a core cybersecurity model that describes three key security objectives:

- **Confidentiality** – protecting sensitive information from unauthorized access.
- **Integrity** – ensuring that information is accurate and cannot be altered improperly.
- **Availability** – ensuring that systems and information remain accessible to authorized users when needed.

These principles are widely used when designing secure information systems (Stallings & Brown, 2018).

---

## Discoveries on Campus

### Confidentiality
Examples of confidentiality protections on campus include:

- Student academic records (grades, transcripts) are protected from unauthorized access.
- University login systems such as student portals and learning management systems require passwords and often **multi-factor authentication (MFA)**.
- Research data is restricted to authorized researchers to prevent data leaks or intellectual property theft.

### Integrity
Integrity ensures that information cannot be altered improperly.

Examples observed on campus include:

- Students cannot modify their own grades within the academic system.
- Examination results are stored securely and protected from tampering.
- Assignment submissions cannot be modified after the deadline in the Learning Management System.

### Availability
Availability ensures that systems and services remain accessible when needed.

Examples include:

- Learning Management Systems (e.g., Blackboard) remain available to students throughout the semester.
- Campus Wi-Fi networks provide continuous access to academic resources.
- Student ID cards with NFC chips allow authorized access to campus buildings and facilities after hours.
