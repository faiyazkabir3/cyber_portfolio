# A15 – Recent Security Incidents

## Activity Description
This activity investigates recent cybersecurity incidents that demonstrate the impact of cyberattacks on organizations and critical infrastructure.

---

## Definition: Security Incidents

Security incidents are events that compromise the confidentiality, integrity, or availability of information systems or sensitive data. These incidents may involve cyberattacks such as ransomware, data breaches, or exploitation of software vulnerabilities that disrupt services or expose confidential information (Whitman & Mattord, 2022).

---

## Discovery

1. **UNFI food-supply disruption attack (2025)** – In June 2025, a cyberattack targeted United Natural Foods Inc. (UNFI), disrupting electronic ordering and delivery systems for a major grocery wholesaler in the United States. This caused shortages in grocery stores and forced retailers to find alternative suppliers, demonstrating the vulnerability of digital supply chains (Fortinet, 2025).

2. **Bank Sepah data breach (2025)** – In March 2025, the hacker group known as “Codebreakers” breached Bank Sepah and exposed approximately one million customer records. The attackers attempted to extort $42 million, highlighting major security risks within the financial sector (Fortinet, 2025).

3. **Marks & Spencer retail cyberattack (2025)** – A social engineering attack conducted during the Easter weekend compromised the Marks & Spencer online retail system. The attack disrupted online shopping services and caused losses estimated at £300 million (Fortinet, 2025).

4. **SAP NetWeaver zero-day vulnerability exploitation (2025)** – In April 2025, a critical remote code execution vulnerability (CVE-2025-31324) in SAP NetWeaver was exploited, allowing attackers to upload malicious web shells and gain unauthorized system access (Fortinet, 2025).

5. **Kettering Health ransomware attack (2025)** – In June 2025, a ransomware attack disrupted internal systems and electronic health records across multiple medical centers, causing medical procedure cancellations (Fortinet, 2025).
