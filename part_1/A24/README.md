# A24 – Teaching Cybersecurity to Family (Phishing Awareness)

---

## Topic Taught: Phishing Awareness

Phishing is a cyberattack where attackers attempt to trick users into revealing sensitive information by impersonating legitimate organizations (Whitman & Mattord, 2022).

---

## What Was Taught

- **Fake emails and messages**  
  Attackers often create emails that look like they come from trusted organizations such as banks or online services.

- **Do not click unknown links**  
  Suspicious links may redirect users to fake websites designed to steal login credentials.

- **Check sender email address**  
  Phishing emails often contain slightly modified or fake email domains.

- **Look for urgency and scam indicators**  
  Messages may include urgent warnings (e.g., account suspension) to pressure users into acting quickly.

---

## Safe Internet Browsing

- Check for **HTTPS (secure connection)** before entering sensitive information  
- Avoid suspicious or unknown websites  
- Do not download files from untrusted sources  

---

## Demonstration

A PowerPoint presentation was used to demonstrate:

- A **fake phishing email example**
- A **real vs fake email comparison**
- Common warning signs of phishing attacks

---

## Outcome

Family members developed a better understanding of phishing attacks and how to identify suspicious emails and websites. They were able to recognize common warning signs and apply safer browsing habits.
