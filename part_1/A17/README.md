# A17 – Different Types of Locks in Use

---

## Definition: Locks

Locks are physical security mechanisms used to control access to buildings, rooms, or secure storage areas. They are part of physical security systems designed to prevent unauthorized entry and protect assets and infrastructure (Stallings & Brown, 2018).

---

Ten different types of locks commonly used in physical security include:

1. **Padlock** – a portable lock commonly used for gates, lockers, and storage units.
2. **Deadbolt lock** – a strong mechanical lock widely used in residential doors.
3. **Combination lock** – a lock that opens using a sequence of numbers instead of a key.
4. **Smart lock** – an electronic lock controlled using mobile applications or digital keys.
5. **Biometric lock** – a lock that uses fingerprint or facial recognition authentication.
6. **Keypad lock** – a lock that requires a PIN code for entry.
7. **Magnetic lock (Maglock)** – an electromagnetic lock commonly used in access control systems.
8. **Cam lock** – a lock commonly used for cabinets, drawers, and lockers.
9. **Mortise lock** – a heavy-duty lock installed within a door structure, often used in commercial buildings.
10. **Rim lock** – a surface-mounted lock typically used on older doors.
