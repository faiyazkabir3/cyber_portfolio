# A2 – Security Concepts Used in Public Spaces

## Activity Description
This activity investigates how cybersecurity and information security principles are applied in public environments. The focus is on identifying how the **CIA triad (Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability)** is implemented in public infrastructure and services.

## Security Concept: CIA Triad
The CIA triad is a foundational cybersecurity model used to guide the design of secure systems and policies. It consists of three key principles:

- **Confidentiality** – protecting sensitive information from unauthorized access.
- **Integrity** – ensuring that information and systems cannot be altered improperly.
- **Availability** – ensuring that systems and services remain accessible to authorized users when required.

These principles are widely applied in both private and public sector systems (Stallings & Brown, 2018).

---

## Discoveries in Public Spaces

### Confidentiality
Confidentiality protects sensitive public data from unauthorized access.

Examples observed in public spaces include:

- CCTV footage is typically restricted to authorized personnel such as law enforcement agencies or city officials.
- Public Wi-Fi networks use encryption protocols such as **WPA3** to protect user data from interception (Australian Cyber Security Centre, 2023).
- Personal information collected through public ticketing systems or transport cards is protected using secure databases and encryption mechanisms (NIST, 2020).

### Integrity
Integrity ensures that public systems and data cannot be modified by unauthorized users.

Examples include:

- Traffic light control systems are protected against unauthorized modifications to prevent malicious interference with traffic flow.
- Public transport payment systems protect fare records from fraudulent modification.
- Government records such as **Tax File Numbers (TFN)** and official citizen data can only be modified by authorized government systems and personnel.

### Availability
Availability ensures that essential public services remain accessible to the population.

Examples include:

- Emergency services such as **000 in Australia** remain accessible at all times to provide assistance during emergencies (Australian Cyber Security Centre, 2023).
- Public transport systems operate continuously according to scheduled availability to ensure mobility for the public.
- Government open data platforms provide public datasets that citizens can access on demand.
